Dal Makhni

Description

Ingredients -

  • 1 cup Black Urad
  • 1/4 cup Rajma
  • 1/4 cup Chana dal
  • Cooking Oil
  • Onion
  • Tomato: Use either freshly chopped ripe tomato or canned crushed tomato
  • Paste
  • Spices: Bay Leaf (tej patta), Cloves (laung), Black Cardamom (elaichi), Cumin (jeera), Dried Fenugreek Leaves
  • Powder: Turmeric Powder, Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder, Coriander Powder, Garam Masala Powder, Asafoetida (hing)
  • Cream : Use light cooking cream with around 25% fat, not heavy or whipping cream. You can substitute cream with full-fat milk, also
  • Herbs & Seasoning: Green Chilli, Ginger, Fresh Coriander, Ginger & Garlic Paste, Salt
  • Garnish: Fresh Coriander, Cream or Butter, Ginger Juliennes

Steps -

  1. Combine all three lentils in a bowl. Rinse them with water 2 – 3 times or until water runs clear. Soak them in clean water for 5 – 6 hours or overnight.
  2. After soaking, pressure cook the lentils. Transfer soaked lentils to a pressure cooker. Add salt, cardamom, bay leaf, cloves, asafoetida, and water. Stir to combine. Pressure Cook lentils over low for 5 – 6 whistles. We want them to break down completely.
  3. Now, let’s make tadka for dal. Heat ghee in a kadhai or heavy-duty casserole. Add cumin, and saute for 10 – 20 seconds. Add finely chopped onion and fry till onion turn light golden.
  4. Once the onion is nicely fried, add ginger and garlic paste, chopped tomato, salt, turmeric, chilli powder, and coriander powder. Fry for 5 – 6 minutes over low heat. The oil should start separating from the masala, and the tomatoes break down completely.
  5. Add tomato paste or ketchup and pressure-cooked dal. Stir to combine. At this stage, if you feel the dal is way too thick, get desired consistency by adding water, as after simmering, it tends to become thicker.
  6. Add cream, Garam Masala powder, kasuri methi, sliced green chillies, julienned ginger, and fresh coriander. Mix nicely. Taste, and if required, add more salt and seasoning.
  7. Now allow the dal to simmer over low heat, stirring at regular intervals for 10 – 15 minutes. This step gives a deep, slow-cooked-like flavour to the dal.
  8. Garnish dal with cream, chopped coriander leaves, and ginger julienned. Dal Makhani is ready to serve.
